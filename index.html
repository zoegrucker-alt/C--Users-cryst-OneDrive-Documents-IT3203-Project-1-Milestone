When we think of the World Wide Web, we often see pages — but beneath them are the invisible roads that make communication possible. This website traces the evolution of HTTP from its earliest days at CERN to modern protocols like HTTP/3 and QUIC.

Why HTTP matters

HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) is more than a technical standard — it is a universal language carrying requests and responses across the web. It allows us to watch videos, pay bills, and communicate in real time. This site highlights how a humble protocol grew into the backbone of modern online life.

Quick facts

Invented: 1989–1991 (Tim Berners-Lee)

Key versions: HTTP/0.9, 1.0, 1.1, 2, 3

Modern focus: speed, resilience, security
